# Tone & Communication Guide

## General Tone

- **Professional but approachable** — Not stiff, not too casual
- **Clear and direct** — Say what you mean without unnecessary padding
- **Empowering** — Help the reader feel capable, not talked down to

## Language Preferences

| Prefer | Avoid |
|--------|-------|
| Simple words | Jargon and buzzwords |
| Active voice | Passive voice |
| Short sentences | Run-on sentences |
| Concrete examples | Abstract statements |
| "You" and "we" | Third-person references to the team |

## By Channel

### Technical Documentation
- Precise and structured
- Code examples where helpful
- No marketing language

### Customer-Facing Content
- Warm and helpful
- Benefit-focused
- Include clear next steps

### Internal Communication
- Direct and efficient
- Context-first, then details
- Action items clearly called out

## Formatting

- Use headings to break up content
- Keep paragraphs to 3 lines or fewer
- Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items
- Bold key terms on first use
- Use tables for comparisons
